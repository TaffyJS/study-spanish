Design a complete modern, engaging, and responsive web application for learning Spanish, suitable for users ranging from complete beginners to conversational and advanced speakers.

## Product concept

Create a Spanish-learning platform that helps users develop vocabulary, grammar, listening, pronunciation, reading, and sentence-building skills through short daily lessons, challenges, quizzes, and spaced repetition.

The experience should feel motivating and friendly rather than academic or overwhelming. Use a clean, modern visual style with soft gradients, rounded cards, subtle shadows, clear typography, friendly illustrations, and smooth micro-interactions.

The website should work well on desktop, tablet, and mobile.

## Target users

* Complete beginners who know no Spanish
* Users with some basic knowledge
* Intermediate learners who want to speak more confidently
* Advanced learners who want to expand their vocabulary and improve fluency

## Visual direction

Use a modern educational app style with:

* A warm and energetic color palette inspired by Spain and Latin America
* A primary color such as coral, orange, or warm red
* Supporting colors such as yellow, deep blue, green, and light neutral backgrounds
* Large rounded cards
* Clear progress indicators
* Friendly icons and illustrations
* Accessible color contrast
* Light mode and optional dark mode
* Smooth hover states, animations, achievement effects, and progress celebrations

Avoid making the interface look childish. It should appeal to teenagers and adults while still feeling playful and welcoming.

## Main navigation

Create a left sidebar on desktop and a bottom navigation bar on mobile.

Include:

* Home
* Learn
* Daily Challenges
* Vocabulary
* Grammar
* Practice
* Progress
* Profile

Display the user’s current streak, level, experience points, and daily goal in the navigation area.

## Onboarding and placement test

Create an onboarding flow that asks:

* Why the user wants to learn Spanish
* Their current Spanish level
* How many minutes they want to study each day
* Whether they prefer Spanish from Spain or Latin American Spanish
* Their learning priorities: travel, conversation, school, work, or general fluency

Offer an optional placement test for users who already know some Spanish.

Allow users to choose a daily vocabulary goal of 10 or 20 new words.

## Home dashboard

Design a motivating dashboard that includes:

* A welcome message using the learner’s name
* Current learning level
* Daily streak
* Experience points
* Weekly activity calendar
* Daily goal progress

* “Continue Learning” button

Make the most important action, “Continue Learning,” visually prominent.

## Learning path

Create a structured learning path from beginner to advanced.

Suggested levels:

### Beginner A1

* Spanish alphabet and pronunciation
* Greetings and introductions
* Numbers, dates, and time
* Common nouns and verbs
* Gender and articles
* Personal pronouns
* Present tense
* Basic sentence construction
* Everyday questions
* Question words
* Family, food, colors, places, and daily activities

### Elementary A2

* Past and future expressions
* Reflexive verbs
* Comparisons
* Directions and travel
* Shopping and restaurants
* Describing people and places
* Longer conversations

### Intermediate B1–B2

* Major past tenses
* Future and conditional tenses
* Subjunctive basics
* Giving opinions
* Storytelling
* Formal and informal speech
* Idioms and natural expressions
* Listening and conversation exercises

### Advanced C1

* Advanced grammar
* Nuanced vocabulary
* Complex sentence structures
* Professional and academic Spanish
* Native-level listening exercises
* Regional expressions
* Longer reading and speaking activities

Display the learning path as connected lesson cards or a visual roadmap. Show completed lessons, current lessons, locked lessons, and milestone tests.

## Lesson experience

Each lesson should include a mix of:

* New vocabulary
* Grammar explanations
* Sentence-building exercises
* Listening exercises
* Pronunciation practice
* Matching activities
* Multiple-choice questions
* Fill-in-the-blank exercises
* Translation exercises
* Short conversations
* End-of-lesson quiz

Keep lessons short and focused, ideally between 5 and 15 minutes.

Display:

* Lesson progress bar
* Remaining questions
* Hearts, energy, or mistake allowance
* Hint button
* Audio replay button
* Explanation button
* Exit lesson option

After each lesson, show:

* Score
* Accuracy
* Experience points earned
* Words learned
* Mistakes to review
* Streak progress
* Next recommended lesson

## Daily challenges

Create exactly five daily challenges each day.

Examples:

1. Learn today’s vocabulary
2. Complete one grammar lesson
3. Answer ten review questions
4. Finish one listening exercise
5. Build five Spanish sentences

Each challenge should show:

* Challenge icon
* Description
* Progress counter
* Experience-point reward
* Completion state
* Claim reward button

Completed challenges should have a satisfying animation or celebration.

## Daily vocabulary and examination system

Every day, users study 10 or 20 new words based on their selected daily goal.

For each word, display:

* Spanish word
* English translation
* Part of speech
* Gender for nouns
* Conjugation information for verbs
* Example sentence
* Sentence translation
* Pronunciation audio
* Phonetic pronunciation
* Related image or icon
* Save to favorites button
* “I know this” and “Study again” options

The next time the user logs in, they should receive an exam on the words studied during their previous session.

The examination should include:

* Spanish-to-English translation
* English-to-Spanish translation
* Listening questions
* Fill-in-the-blank sentences
* Multiple-choice questions
* Word matching
* Typing exercises

After the exam, divide words into:

* Mastered
* Almost learned
* Needs more practice

Use spaced repetition to automatically bring back difficult words after one day, three days, seven days, fourteen days, and thirty days.

* Subject pronouns
* Adjective agreement
* Normal statement structure
* Negative sentence structure
* Question structure
* Question words
* Direct and indirect objects
* Common prepositions

* Present progressive
* Preterite

* Future tense

## Important UX requirements

* Make the experience simple for first-time users
* Keep each screen focused on one primary action
* Use clear progress feedback
* Make buttons large and accessible
* Include loading, empty, error, locked, completed, and success states
* Use encouraging language after mistakes
* Never make wrong answers feel discouraging
* Provide explanations after incorrect answers
* Allow users to review every mistake
* Save lesson progress automatically
* Make all major interactions keyboard-accessible
* Include captions and transcripts for audio exercises
